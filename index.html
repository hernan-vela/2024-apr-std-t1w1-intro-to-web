Imagine, if you can, writing something that will one day be known universally as “the Great American Novel.”

Mark Twain did just that with Adventures of Huckleberry Finn which was published in 1885. It's predecessor, The Adventures of Tom Sawyer, came out nine years earlier and both became worldwide favourites and are still read today over a century later. Mark Twain is, deservedly, still known as one of his country's greatest writers which is remarkable considering that his work was mostly around the end of the 19th century. The celebrated writer William Faulkner called him “the father of American literature.”
